I'm a passionate programmer and I love every bit of my craft. I love the way building things make me feel and how I can continually learn new things, every second. When I have a problem, I can create the solution. That feeling of empowerment is the reason I'm a monthly donator to Wikipedia, Khan Academy, and EEF.

I have six main passions in life (not counting people):

Reading

I love reading. It can be books, articles, or even ads on the subway (I hate ads, but I like to get a feel of trends). I try to read 40 books a year, but I cheat by inluding audiobooks and graphic novels (or comics with over 500 pages).

I've requested a simpler API from Goodreads so I can create my own widget and show what I'm reading, but that might take a while. Their current solution is based on OAuth, which basically means you would see the books you're currently reading, not mine. Boo.

It's hard to say what's my favourite book of all time, but the ones that got me started are Swedish ones when I was in my teens. I read pretty much all Bert's Dagböcker, and later I read Jan Guillou's Ondskan and the Templar-books.

You can follow my reading on GoodReads.

Coding

I love coding, and currently I'm focusing on... Here I'd like to grab my activity on GitHub and post it, but it wouldn't be fair because I use a lot of different services just to try them out. Like jsbin, codepen, koding, codio, and others I've forgotten.

I've involved myself in OSS since it became a thing. My earliest contribution was to TinyXML, remember that one? Now in later days, here are some projects I'm proud to have contributed to:

  • Twitter Bootstrap: I made the move from makefile to GruntJS.
  • Holder.js: Replaced makefile with GruntJS.
  • Raphäel: Yepp, GruntJS instead of makefile.

My most popular "creation" is probably ThumbView. You know how you preview images in Explorer/Finder? Well, way back in Win XP it didn't support the popular TGA and PNG files - so I added that. So far both versions have been downloaded over a quarter-million times in total. I've attempted to create sequels to it, but Windows damn important UAC stops every attempt so far.

You can follow my coding on Github and Codepen.

Thanks to the inspiration of great friends and teachers, I've started giving talks and mentorships. I try to organize various activities where learning is the focus. Some of these activities are completely on my spare time, like these talks I've given:

I also host various "haxx" as me and my friends call it. It's mini-hackathons, usually at my place. We're social coders I guess, because we code best close to other people without necessarily coding with them.

At work I can't help trying to engage people and spread the love of code. Besides giving presentations I've organized "learning at lunch", book club, hackathons, code dojos, and my biggest endeavour: Mad Mondays. Mad Mondays occur on the first Monday every month and it's a full day of focused working on a new concept with up to four co-workers. So far we've done Windows 8 slate prototypes, phone app concepts, pure concepts, and even had Tobii visit to create usages for eye-tracking.

I love writing (other than code) and it happens that I blog when I think I've done something notable of other peoples attention. Otherwise I do a bit of tweeting, although mostly retweets I guess.

You can follow my thoughts on my blog, Twitter, and Slideshare.

Working

I have a BSc in Computer Engineering, Real-Time Systems, and an unfinished MSc in Computer Engineering, Human-Computer Interaction. All from the Royal Institute of Technology, Sweden.

I've had summer jobs and the like for as long as I was legally allowed to work, but my "career" started in 2007 when I founded my own company and worked as a freelancer.

In 2009 I decided to stop running everything myself and joined a company. Since then I've moved around a bit and I'm currently UX Developer Lead at Avanade.

You can follow my career on LinkedIn and Careers 2.0.
